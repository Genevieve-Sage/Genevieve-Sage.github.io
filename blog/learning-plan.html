My Learning Plan

Foundations Learning Plan

The learning plan describes how I will operate in the context of the Dev Academy Foundations and Bootcamp programme. I am fully committed to doing what I say I will do. If I say what, how and when I will do something, I will deliver it as promised. If anything changes with that, I negotiate this in a timely and professional manner.

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My longterm goal is to become a senior web developer and perhaps start my own business. I would like to start out working for a smaller organisation where I can have some solid mentoring for a few months. Then I would like to develop more of my technical skills so that I can work at a higher level. I will continue with my professional development, to stay relevant and up-to-date with the latest trends.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning

I see the following as my strengths: I am very determined to succeed and even though I am just starting out in this career I will make every effort to learn as fast as I can and as well as I can. - I am persistent. - I am generally a positive person and I work well in groups most of the time. - I have a lot of experience at life. My limitations: I do have a lot to learn in this area. I am trying to learn fast, however this subject area is one where you can not just skip information, you need to know it all and this is very time consuming. I have a life outside of EDA and need to make sure I balance my studies with spending time with family and friends. I do not like to ask for help, but I am making myself vulnerable in this area.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives

I am making a commitment to honour what I say I will do and complete tasks in a timely manner and well. If I don’t understand something I will research it and if I need further clarification I will ask my cohort or facilitators for help. I have set myself a target to work Monday to Friday as a working week - 40 hours at least. If I have to work weekends I will, but I will make sure I have at least one day off per week. I will respect all the people I come into contact with and value their input. Having been a teacher and working with student’s I know that it is essential to consider the safety of the people around me. That means looking after peoples hearts and minds.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

I will speak with someone and let them know if things are getting on top of me. I will have some time out and get a bit of me time, i.e go to a movie, go shopping. I enjoy exercising as I find it is a release for me and does relieve tension, so I will make sure I keep up with that. I will try to come back with a fresh mind after having a short breather.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I am committed to receiving help in a timely manner if things start to get on top of me.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to be able to assist me if I am having difficulties within the learning environment. Sometimes things outside the classroom can also cause stress and I hope the facilitators will be understanding of these if they should arise. I would like them to also celebrate the wins and just generally be a positive and encouraging bunch.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It’s important to plan these in.

If I have any appointments, I will ensure I tell the school especially when I am on bootcamp and ensure I do so in a timely manner. Otherwise, I am really enjoying the challenge and I am all in.

Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I am hoping to use these skills I gain during the course to move into a technology based career. I would like to start out in web design. I would like to have an impact with my creativity within the software development arena, that is to build beautifully grafted, easy to use websites or learning pathways for students. Perhaps I will use my talents to upgrade the New Zealand Curriculum in some way. My destination is still in it's formation stage.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My greatest strengths in Bootcamp will be my resilience and drive, I like completion. I think my limitations will likely be how quickly I pick up the knowledge.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think my biggest non-technical challenge will be having to spend the first few weeks of Bootcamp working from home instead of in the classroom with people. I enjoy being around people and it is also easier to bounce ideas off each other if you are in the same room.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

It is always beneficial to continue to hone your communication skills. Anything to do with improved listening and communication would be awesome.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect the Bootcamp team to be available, to be friendly and knowledgeable. That they will freely share their knowledge and not be too judgemental when something takes a while to click.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

While on Bootcamp I expect myself to show up ready and willing to get on with the day's tasks to the best of my ability. I will be reliable and also let others know when I am finding problem solving a challenge. I will put my best foot forward and be kind and considerate at all times. I will study as much as I can so that I can offer as much as possible to my peers.
